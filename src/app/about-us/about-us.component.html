About GRP

Originating from Guangzhou Railway Mechanical School, which was built in May 1974, and affiliated to Guangzhou Railway (Group) Corporation (today’s China Railway Guangzhou Group, abbreviated as Guangzhou Railway),Guangzhou Railway Polytechnic (GRP) was officially founded in 2000 upon approval. In September 2004, the People’s Government of Guangzhou Municipality took over the administration over the GRP.


GRP focuses on students’ employment and delivers fruitful results. It recruits students from 20 provinces (regions) such as Guangdong, Hunan, Jiangxi, Sichuan, Henan, and Inner Mongolia; graduates can find best-fit jobs. The average initial employment rate of graduates is 98.51%, and the average employment counterpart rate is 93.30%. The proportion of “Order-based Employment” by regional leading enterprises in rail transit reaches over 65% and the satisfaction rate of enterprises reaches 98%. The employment counterpart rate and salary level of graduates are among the best in Guangdong province. Xie Dongtian, the winner of the 2012 National May 1st Labor Medal of Shenzhen Metro Group Co., Ltd., Wu Huachuang, the winner of the “Locomotives Medal” of the China Railway Guangzhou Group Co., Ltd. in 2014, Lai Zetuan, the winner of the “National Railway Model Worker” of the China Railway Guangzhou Group Co., Ltd. in 2014, Xu Zhibiao, the winner of the 2014 National May 1st Labor Medal of the Guangzhou Metro Group, and Chen Chengyi, the representative of the 19th National Congress of Fujian Province and the winner of the National Model Worker, Hong Haiyang, the craftsman of China Railway and the leader in the New Century National Hundred, Thousand, and Ten Thousand Talents Project, and Lin Zhanxiong, the winner of champion in 2019 China Vocational Skills Competition are all alumni of our college.


GRP adheres to the strategy of internationalization at a higher level. The college is deeply committed to serving the national “Belt and Road Initiative” and the strategy of high-speed railway “going global”. With the Industry-education Integration Alliance, we have been actively involved in expanding international cooperation platform for rail transit by connecting with four regions: the Commonwealth of Independent States, Southeast Asia, Africa, and Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area. With the approval of the Education Bureau of Guangdong Province, GRP signed a cooperation agreement with the Belarusian State University of Transport to co-offer the Railway Traffic Operation and Management Program (a full-time higher vocational training program). In addition, GRP has officially enrolled overseas students from Laos, Malaysia and other countries, taking the lead among vocational colleges administered by Guangzhou Municipal Government for teaching full-time overseas students.


GRP insists on moral education and achieves remarkable results. Under the guidance of Xi Jinping’s Thought on Socialism with Chinese Characteristics for a New Era, GRP will persist on the path of putting moral education at its core. With the orientation of “relying on the industry, taking root in Guangzhou, influencing the country and facing the world”, and the philosophy of “lifting the school to a new high by valuing quality, talents, uniqueness and culture”, GRP follows the 2035 Education Modernization Plan and contributes to the upgrading of national rail transit industry and the development of Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area. It aims to build the modern vocational education system that equally values academic education and vocational training by improving its internal management system, deepening the reform on grooming inter-disciplinary technical talents by means of industry-education integration, and developing an array of programs that feature a lead by specialized programs, a support from key disciplines and coordinated development between them, all of which will constitute a high-quality technical talent training system characterized with work-integrated learning and the combination of action and knowledge. GRP will continue to strive for building a high-level vocational college with Chinese characteristics and fair international influence.
